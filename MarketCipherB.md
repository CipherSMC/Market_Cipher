# 📊 Trading Guide: Market Cipher B

Market Cipher B is a comprehensive "all-in-one" oscillator. Instead of looking at price alone, it tracks the **momentum** of the market and the **flow of money** into and out of an asset.

[Cipher B: Indicator](https://www.tradingview.com/script/5BPJmxuY-Cipher-B-Divergences-Cipher-SMC/)

## 1. Understanding the Components

To trade this indicator, you must understand its five primary layers:

1.  **WaveTrend (The Waves):** The blue and purple areas. They represent momentum. When waves are high, the asset is overbought; when they are low, it is oversold.
2.  **Money Flow Index (MFI):** The green and red shaded area in the background. 
    *   **Green:** Money is flowing **into** the asset (Bullish).
    *   **Red:** Money is flowing **out** of the asset (Bearish).
3.  **VWAP (The Yellow/White Line):** Represents the Volume Weighted Average Price momentum. It acts as a leading "trigger" for momentum shifts.
4.  **RSI Lines:** Tracks relative strength. When RSIs are tightly "clustered" together, a big move is usually coming.
5.  **Signal Dots:** 
    *   🟢 **Green Dot:** Bullish momentum cross.
    *   🔴 **Red Dot:** Bearish momentum cross.
    *   🟡 **Gold Dot:** A high-probability bullish divergence signal.

## 2. Core Trading Strategies

### A. The "Bread and Butter" Bullish Setup
This is the most common setup for long entries. Look for confluence (multiple things happening at once):
1.  **Oversold Waves:** The WaveTrend waves should be near or below the **-53 or -60** levels.
2.  **Green Money Flow:** Ideally, the MFI area is turning green or increasing in size.
3.  **The Trigger:** A **Green Dot** appears as the fast wave (WT1) crosses above the slow wave (WT2).
4.  **VWAP Confirmation:** The yellow VWAP line should be curling upward.

### B. The "Blood in the Water" Bearish Setup
For shorting or exiting longs:
1.  **Overbought Waves:** WaveTrend is near or above the **+53 or +60** levels.
2.  **Red Money Flow:** MFI is turning red, indicating big players are exiting positions.
3.  **The Trigger:** A **Red Dot** appears at the peak of a wave.
4.  **VWAP Confirmation:** The yellow VWAP line is curling downward toward the zero line.

---

## 3. Advanced Technique: Divergences
Divergences are the most powerful signals in Market Cipher B. They occur when the **Price** and the **Waves** disagree.

*   **Bullish Divergence:** Price makes a **Lower Low**, but the WaveTrend makes a **Higher Low**. This suggests that even though price is falling, the selling pressure is exhausting.
*   **Bearish Divergence:** Price makes a **Higher High**, but the WaveTrend makes a **Lower High**. This suggests that the move upward is losing "fuel" and a reversal is likely.

*Note: The purple triangles in the VMC version highlight these automatically.*

---

## 4. The "Money Flow" Rule
**Never trade against the Money Flow on high timeframes.**
*   If the MFI is a thick **Green** block, be very cautious about shorting, even if you see a red dot.
*   If the MFI is a thick **Red** block, be very cautious about buying, even if you see a green dot.

The best trades occur when the **Dot color matches the Money Flow color.**

---

## 5. Pro Tips for Success

*   **Timeframe Confluence:** A Green Dot on the **4-hour** chart is significantly more powerful than a Green Dot on the **1-minute** chart. Always check the higher timeframe trend first.
*   **The Zero Line:** The center line (0) is a "gravity" point. When VWAP or WaveTrend crosses the zero line, it often indicates a transition from a relief rally to a full-blown trend.
*   **Don't Chase:** If you see a Green Dot but the waves are already halfway to the overbought zone, you have missed the entry. Wait for the next cycle.
*   **Combine with Market Structure:** Use Market Cipher B alongside **Support and Resistance** levels. A Green Dot appearing exactly on a major support level is a "Sniper" entry.

### ⚠️ Risk Disclaimer
*Market Cipher B is a lagging indicator based on historical data. No signal is 100% accurate. Always use a Stop Loss and never risk more than 1-2% of your account on a single trade.*
